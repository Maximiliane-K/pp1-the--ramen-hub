Sobahouse Konjiki Hototogisu

Being one of the Michelin starred Ramen shops in Tokyo Konjiki Hototogisu has a presence of its own.
Their specialty bowl is a salty Ramen with red sea bream, clams and a dashi from dried produce. When served it is topped with truffle sauce and the fragrance of porcini oil.

As a additional topping you can get a slice of their characteristic roasted Iberico pork. All this rich flavour is then being garnished with edible winter cherry sauce.
The combination is so unique and make this a great experience. As a bonus this is probably one of the most affordable Michelin starred meals you can grab.

Sosakumenkobo Nakiryu

The signature Ramen bowls of Kazumasa Saito are his dandan noodles and soy-sauce ramen which secrets to their insanely good taste is the broth.
A mix of chicken, beef bones, oysters and kombu all cooked in one pot.
With every ingredient's umami adding its own layer for this deeply flavorful bowl of pure taste.

At Nakiryu chef Saito draws to his experience in China and seasons his dandan noodles with home-grown sesame paste and chilli oil.
The flavours that are in just one bowl make so much sense and you will understand why he earned himself a Michelin star.
The dandan are the house speciality but the soy-sauce Ramen is not less flavorful. It is made from multiple soy sauces which make a rich, weighty and though light flavour.

Ichiran

ICHIRAN’s menu is focused only on tonkotsu Ramen.
With focus being their mantra the dining experience is also all about focusing on your bowl of richness in the setting of your very own booth.
It’s a must try and you can find them scattered around Tokyo.

The broth is made with natural pork-bone which is skimmed to perfection so that the ultimate flavor of pork is drawn out.
The special raw noodle is made from a unique blend of flours to make the synergy between noodle and soup perfect.
All this is then topped off with the original spicy red sauce.

Fuunji

Fuunji (風雲児) has a specialty called Tsukemen style Ramen. It is located in Shinjuku Tokyo’s hub for Ramen shops.
Fuunji probably is one of the most popular restaurants in Tokyo and doesn’t only attract foreign Ramen lovers but also a lot of locals.

Tsukemen (dipping noodles) is a different style of Ramen. The noodles and soup are served seperatly.
The stock is made out of chicken and fish and has the most rich and aromatic taste you can imagine.

Hayashida

With Ramen Hayashida being my personal favorite I’m unsure why they don’t have a Michelin star.
But who know maybe they’re cooking one up just right now.
They have a few must tries. Defnitly their classic duck and chicken broth that is served since 2017.

The trending bowl is their seafood broth based on the famous Nodoguro.
The fish is typically served as sashimi or braised but Hayashida figured out how to make an amazing broth with it and this surely deserves at least one Michelin star.
